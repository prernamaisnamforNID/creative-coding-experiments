Threads…caught me…against my will….
But circumstances held me to threads and they won me over.
Anni Albers in her weaving studio · Black Mountain College · 1937
Annelise Fleischmann was born on 12 June 1899, in Berlin, into a prosperous Jewish family. Drawn to art from childhood, she trained under Impressionist painter Martin Brandenburg before the pull of something more structured — more tactile — led her elsewhere.

In 1922 she enrolled at the Bauhaus in Weimar. The school's masters, believing women belonged at the loom rather than in architecture or painting, directed her to the weaving workshop. What began as institutional constraint became a lifelong vocation.

She later said she had no idea what weaving even was when she arrived. Within two years she was the most technically gifted student in the workshop.
Life & Legacy · Timeline
1899
Born Annelise Fleischmann in Berlin into a prosperous Jewish family.
1922
Enrolls at the Bauhaus in Weimar. Directed to the weaving workshop.
1925
Marries the painter Josef Albers. Bauhaus moves to Dessau. Anni appointed junior master of the weaving workshop.
1933
Bauhaus closes under Nazi pressure. Anni and Josef emigrate to the USA, joining Black Mountain College, North Carolina.
1949
Solo retrospective at the Museum of Modern Art, New York — the first dedicated to a textile artist. Moves to New Haven, Connecticut.
1950s – 60s
Travels extensively in Mexico and Peru. Begins printmaking, producing over 100 prints. Publishes On Designing (1959) and On Weaving (1965).
1972
Josef Albers dies. Anni continues to weave and exhibit internationally.
1994
Anni Albers dies in Orange, Connecticut, aged 94. Her work is held by MoMA, the Tate, the Bauhaus-Archiv, and institutions worldwide.
